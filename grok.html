The Age of Enlightenment in the Heart of the Swiss Alps (1970–2026)

Introduction

Maharishi Mahesh Yogi, founder of the Transcendental Meditation (TM) movement, established Seelisberg as a global hub in the early 1970s. Located high above Lake Lucerne in Canton Uri, this picturesque Swiss village became the international headquarters for TM from approximately 1971–1983.

Maharishi chose the former Hotel Sonnenberg as the center, transforming it into the Maharishi European Research University (MERU) and later adding the Maharishi Ayurveda Health Centre.

Timeline

Early 1970s

Maharishi acquires Hotel Sonnenberg; Seelisberg becomes the world headquarters for the TM movement.

1975

Founding of MERU for scientific research on TM and higher states of consciousness. Inauguration of the "Dawn of the Age of Enlightenment."

1976–1983

Peak years; Maharishi resides and teaches in Seelisberg. Introduction of advanced programs like TM-Sidhi (including Yogic Flying).

Early 1980s

Launch of Maharishi Ayurveda in Seelisberg – the birthplace of this revived system outside India.

1990

Global headquarters moves to Vlodrop, Netherlands.

2025

The property is sold, marking the end of an era, though TM-related activities continue in the region.

Key Events and Legacy

Seelisberg was the epicenter of TM's "golden age." Thousands attended courses, teacher training, and personal lectures by Maharishi. It hosted international conferences blending ancient Vedic knowledge with modern science.

The serene Alpine location inspired major developments in the movement, including global programs aimed at creating world peace through collective meditation.

"Seelisberg is a place of peace and the most creative place in Central Europe." – Maharishi Mahesh Yogi

Today, while the main facilities have transitioned (with the Ayurveda Health Centre closing in 2025), the site remains a place of profound historical significance for TM practitioners worldwide.

The Location

Seelisberg offers breathtaking views over Lake Lucerne and the surrounding Alps – a setting that perfectly complemented the pursuit of inner peace.
