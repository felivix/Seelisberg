Historical Timeline

The journey of transformation and global impact

1957-1958

The Beginning

Maharishi Mahesh Yogi established his first international meditation academy in Seelisberg, Switzerland. This mountain retreat became the foundation for spreading Transcendental Meditation to the Western world.

1960s

Global Expansion

From the serene Swiss Alps, Maharishi's teachings spread across Europe and America. The movement gained momentum, attracting seekers from around the world to the peaceful sanctuary of Seelisberg.

1970s

Scientific Validation

Research into Transcendental Meditation began to validate its benefits. The Seelisberg center became a hub for both spiritual practice and scientific inquiry into consciousness and meditation.

1980s

Educational Initiatives

Maharishi University of Management concepts were developed, incorporating meditation into educational frameworks. Seelisberg served as a model for consciousness-based education.

Present Day

Continuing Legacy

Today, the Seelisberg center continues to inspire practitioners worldwide. The movement has touched millions of lives, maintaining its commitment to individual and collective consciousness development.

Enduring Impact

The movement's contributions to humanity

Transcendental Meditation

Millions worldwide practice TM, benefiting from reduced stress, improved clarity, and enhanced well-being—a technique refined and shared from the peaks of Seelisberg.

Scientific Research

Over 600 scientific studies have explored meditation's effects on health, cognition, and consciousness, validating ancient wisdom with modern methodology.

Peace Initiatives

Group meditation projects aimed at creating coherence and reducing conflict have been conducted globally, inspired by the vision cultivated in Seelisberg.

Cultural Bridge

The movement served as a bridge between Eastern spiritual wisdom and Western scientific inquiry, creating dialogue and understanding across cultures.
