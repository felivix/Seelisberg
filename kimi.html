A tiny village, a giant leap

In 1968 Maharishi Mahesh Yogi chose the lakeside hamlet of Seelisberg, Switzerland, as his world headquarters. From a modest hotel overlooking Lake Lucerne he trained thousands of TM teachers, launched the World Plan Executive Council, and established MERU (Maharishi European Research University) to study the science of consciousness. For more than a decade the quiet alpine air buzzed with lectures, courses, and the hope of creating world peace through meditation. Today the centre still welcomes practitioners, though on a gentler scale, and Seelisberg remains a place of pilgrimage for TM meditators worldwide. Source: switzerlandinsound.com

Key moments

  • 1968 – Maharishi stops public touring and opens global HQ in Seelisberg to train TM teachers. [^6^]
  • 1969 – Science of Creative Intelligence course inaugurated at Stanford and rolled out to 25 U.S. campuses. [^6^]
  • 1972 – World Plan Executive Council founded in Seelisberg; goal: 3 600 TM centres worldwide. [^4^]
  • 1975-78 – MERU conducts pioneering studies on TM; Swiss radio interviews Maharishi on future of the Movement. [^5^]
  • Late-1970s – Local authorities ask Maharishi to limit entourage; he quietly relocates main activities to the Netherlands. [^5^]
  • Today – Seelisberg remains an active but smaller campus; facilities include Mothers Divine programme and an Ayurvedic clinic. [^5^]

Glimpse of the era

Living heritage

Whether you are a long-time meditator or simply curious, Seelisberg offers panoramic views, peaceful forest paths, and the gentle resonance of a place where thousands first learned to “dive within”. The Maharishi Medical Centre continues to provide authentic Ayurvedic treatments, and short retreats in the Mothers Divine programme keep the flame of silence glowing in the alpine quiet.
