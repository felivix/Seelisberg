Maharishi's Movement in Seelisberg

Discover the profound history of Maharishi Mahesh Yogi's spiritual movement and its establishment in the serene Swiss village of Seelisberg, overlooking Lake Lucerne.

Explore the History

Historical Background

The Swiss Foundation of a Global Movement

In the late 1970s, Maharishi Mahesh Yogi, the founder of Transcendental Meditation, chose the picturesque village of Seelisberg in Switzerland as the European headquarters for his worldwide spiritual movement. Nestled in the Swiss Alps with breathtaking views of Lake Lucerne, Seelisberg provided the perfect tranquil environment for meditation and spiritual study.

The movement established the "Maharishi European Research University" (MERU) in Seelisberg, which became a center for teaching Transcendental Meditation and training teachers in the technique. This location served as a hub for spreading Maharishi's teachings throughout Europe and played a significant role in the global expansion of his movement.

Seelisberg's natural beauty and peaceful atmosphere aligned perfectly with Maharishi's emphasis on connecting with nature and creating an ideal environment for personal development and higher consciousness.

Historical Timeline

1958

Beginning of Global Movement

Maharishi Mahesh Yogi begins his first world tour, teaching Transcendental Meditation and establishing the foundation of what would become a global spiritual movement.

1975

Expansion to Europe

Maharishi's movement gains significant popularity in Europe, leading to the search for a permanent European headquarters that would eventually be established in Seelisberg.

1978

Establishment in Seelisberg

The Maharishi European Research University (MERU) is officially established in Seelisberg, Switzerland, becoming the European center for Transcendental Meditation training and research.

1980s

Growth and Development

The Seelisberg center expands, attracting thousands of visitors and students from across Europe and beyond. It becomes a hub for conferences, teacher training, and advanced meditation programs.

2008

Maharishi's Passing

Maharishi Mahesh Yogi passes away, but his movement continues to thrive with Seelisberg remaining an important center for the global organization.

Present Day

Continued Legacy

The Seelisberg center continues to operate, hosting meditation retreats, training programs, and serving as a reminder of Maharishi's vision for world peace through individual enlightenment.

Core Philosophy

Transcendental Meditation and World Peace

At the heart of Maharishi's movement was Transcendental Meditation (TM), a simple, natural technique practiced for 20 minutes twice daily while sitting comfortably with eyes closed. Maharishi taught that through TM, individuals could access deeper levels of consciousness, reducing stress and promoting holistic development.

Maharishi's vision extended beyond individual benefits to global transformation. He proposed that if enough people practiced TM together in groups, it would create a "Maharishi Effect" - a measurable influence of reduced crime, violence, and social stress in the surrounding community, ultimately contributing to world peace.

The Seelisberg center was designed as an ideal environment for this practice, with its natural beauty and peaceful atmosphere enhancing the meditation experience and serving as a model for creating harmony between human life and nature.
