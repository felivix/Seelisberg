The Capital of the Age of Enlightenment

Exploring the history of Maharishi Mahesh Yogi's global headquarters above Lake Lucerne.

A Mountain Sanctuary

For over two decades, the quiet Swiss village of Seelisberg was the epicenter of a global movement. High above the Rütli meadow, the historic Grand Hotel Sonnenberg was transformed into the international headquarters for Maharishi Mahesh Yogi and the Transcendental Meditation organization.

From this vantage point, major international courses were held, global initiatives were launched, and the "World Government of the Age of Enlightenment" was established, drawing thousands of seekers, scientists, and leaders to the Swiss Alps.

Chronology of an Era

Early 1970s

Acquisition of the Sonnenberg

The TM movement acquires the historic, albeit aging, Grand Hotel Sonnenberg. Its isolated location and stunning vistas over Lake Lucerne make it an ideal location for deep meditation courses and administrative focus.

1975

The Dawn of the Age of Enlightenment

Maharishi inaugurates the "Dawn of the Age of Enlightenment" in Seelisberg. This marks a shift towards large-scale global initiatives aimed at creating societal coherence.

Late 1970s

Peak Activity & Global Assemblies

Seelisberg bustles with activity. The hotel is often filled to capacity with international course participants. The "World Government" is formally established here, issuing its own currency and passports as symbolic gestures of a borderless world of consciousness.

1980s

The Shift to Vlodrop

While Seelisberg remained significant, the movement's central focus began to gradually shift towards the new campus in Vlodrop, Netherlands, which offered more space for expansion. Seelisberg remained a key location for specific high-level assemblies.

Post-1990

End of an Era

The movement eventually divested the Sonnenberg property. The golden era of Seelisberg as the center of the TM world concluded, leaving behind a rich historical legacy in the Swiss mountains.

The Legacy of the Place

Echoes of Enlightenment

Today, the physical presence of the movement in Seelisberg has faded, but its historical footprint remains. For thousands of people around the world, Seelisberg represents a pivotal time of intense learning, hope for global peace, and proximity to Maharishi.

This archive serves to document the unique convergence of Swiss alpine history and the peak years of a global spiritual phenomenon.
