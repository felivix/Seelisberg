The Capital of the Age of Enlightenment

Exploring the history of Maharishi Mahesh Yogi's global headquarters high above Lake Lucerne.

A Mountain Sanctuary

For over two decades, the quiet Swiss village of Seelisberg served as the unlikely epicenter of a global spiritual movement. High above the historic Rütli meadow, the vast Grand Hotel Sonnenberg was transformed into the international headquarters for Maharishi Mahesh Yogi.

From this vantage point, major international teacher training courses were held, global initiatives were launched, and the "World Government of the Age of Enlightenment" was formally established, drawing thousands of seekers, scientists, and leaders to the crisp air of the Swiss Alps.

Chronology of an Era

c. 1972

Acquisition of the Sonnenberg

The movement acquires the massive, historic Grand Hotel Sonnenberg. Its isolated location offering stunning vistas over Lake Lucerne made it an ideal location for deep meditation courses and focused administrative work.

1975

Inaugurating the "Dawn"

Maharishi inaugurates the "Dawn of the Age of Enlightenment" in Seelisberg. This marked a significant pivot towards large-scale global initiatives aimed at creating societal coherence through group meditation practice.

Late 1970s

The Peak Years

Seelisberg bustles with intense activity. The hotel is filled to capacity with international course participants. The "World Government" is established here, issuing symbolic currency and passports reflecting a borderless world of consciousness.

1980s - 1990s

The Gradual Shift

While Seelisberg remained symbolically important, the operational center gradually shifted towards the new campus in Vlodrop, Netherlands (MERU), which offered more room for expansion.

Echoes of Enlightenment

Today, the physical presence of the movement in the village has largely faded, but its historical footprint remains significant. The location itself, perched above the birthplace of the Swiss Confederation (the Rütli), added a layer of historical gravity to Maharishi's mission.

For thousands around the world, Seelisberg represents a pivotal time of intense learning, hope for global peace, and proximity to Maharishi during the movement's most expansive phase.
