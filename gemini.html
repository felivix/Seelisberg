The Capital of the Age of Enlightenment

Seelisberg, Switzerland (1972–1992)

A Global Headquarters

In the early 1970s, the Transcendental Meditation movement acquired the majestic Grand Hotel Sonnenberg, located high above Lake Lucerne (Vierwaldstättersee). The location was not accidental; perched directly above the Rütli Meadow—the historic birthplace of Switzerland—it offered symbolic weight to Maharishi Mahesh Yogi's mission.

From this alpine sanctuary, Maharishi inaugurated the "Dawn of the Age of Enlightenment" in 1975. The hotel became a bustling hub where scientists, educators, and thousands of course participants gathered to explore consciousness.

Maharishi Mahesh Yogi, circa 1979

Key Milestones

1972

Acquisition

The TM movement purchases the Victorian-era Grand Hotel Sonnenberg. It becomes the international administrative center.

1975

The Dawn

Maharishi formally inaugurates the "Age of Enlightenment." The location is dubbed the "Capital" of this new era.

1980

Vedic Science

The first "Vedic Science" courses and the establishment of the Maharishi European Research University (MERU) take place here.

The view from Seelisberg looking over Lake Lucerne.

Legacy of the Mountain

While the movement eventually shifted its central operations to Vlodrop, Netherlands in the 1990s, Seelisberg retains a mythical status among practitioners. The combination of the sheer natural beauty of the Swiss Alps and the intense period of activity in the 1970s made it a unique chapter in spiritual history.
