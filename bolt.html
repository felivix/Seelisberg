The History

In 1955, Maharishi Mahesh Yogi emerged from years of study with his master, Swami Brahmananda Saraswati (Guru Dev), in the Himalayas. After a period of silence and contemplation in Uttar Kashi, he traveled south through India, eventually arriving in Kerala where he began teaching meditation.

In late 1957, Maharishi journeyed to the West, arriving in Seelisberg, Switzerland. This picturesque alpine village, perched high above Lake Lucerne, would become the birthplace of a global spiritual movement. The pristine mountain environment provided the perfect setting for deep meditation and profound spiritual teachings.

On January 1, 1958, in Seelisberg, Maharishi formally established the Spiritual Regeneration Movement, marking the beginning of his mission to bring the benefits of Transcendental Meditation to people everywhere. This moment represented a pivotal point in modern spiritual history.

Seelisberg: A Sacred Location

Seelisberg is a small mountain resort village in the canton of Uri, Switzerland. Situated at an elevation of approximately 800 meters above Lake Lucerne, it offers breathtaking panoramic views of the surrounding Alps.

The area is steeped in Swiss history and legend, located near the Rütli meadow where the Swiss Confederation was founded. This combination of natural beauty, historical significance, and spiritual atmosphere made it an ideal location for Maharishi's work.

The silence and purity of the mountain environment, combined with the inspiring views, created the perfect conditions for meditation and spiritual awakening. Many early students of Transcendental Meditation traveled to Seelisberg to learn directly from Maharishi in this sacred setting.

The movement grows exponentially, with teacher training courses, research validation, and millions learning Transcendental Meditation worldwide.

The Legacy

Transcendental Meditation

The meditation technique Maharishi brought to the world from Seelisberg has been learned by millions of people. It is characterized by its simplicity, naturalness, and effectiveness. Practiced twice daily for 20 minutes, TM allows the mind to settle inward beyond thought to experience pure consciousness.

Scientific Validation

Over 600 scientific studies conducted at more than 250 universities and research institutions have documented the benefits of Transcendental Meditation, including reduced stress, improved health, enhanced brain function, and increased happiness.

Educational Institutions

The movement led to the establishment of educational institutions worldwide, including Maharishi International University in Iowa, USA, and similar institutions in other countries, where consciousness-based education is integrated with modern academic disciplines.

Continuing Influence

The seed planted in Seelisberg continues to flourish today. The principles and practices established there have influenced millions of lives, contributing to individual development, world peace initiatives, and the revival of ancient Vedic knowledge in modern context.
